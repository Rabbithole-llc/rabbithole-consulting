You're present on every major platform a traveler checks — but you capture none of them directly. There's no booking engine on your site, and your site links to none of your own listings. So the platforms hold the guest relationship, set the ranking, and own the data. Your reputation is doing the selling; the platforms are keeping the customer.

Reputation & guest sentiment

4.6★ across 470 reviews is a strong, established reputation — reinforced across TripAdvisor, Booking, Expedia, and Yelp. The recent sample is polarized, though: alongside the 5★ reviews there's a recent 1★ and 2★. Recent low reviews carry outsized weight in how Google and the OTAs rank you, so a thoughtful public reply to each is worth doing now.

What needs attention — in priority order

You can't take a booking on your own site

Critical

Why it matters. Every guest goes through TripAdvisor, Booking, or Expedia — you never own the guest, their details, or the terms, and you're fully dependent on those platforms' rankings and rules.

The fix. Add a booking engine (Cloudbeds, Little Hotelier, Lodgify) so guests can reserve directly — live within days.

Your website sends guests to other hotels

Critical

Why it matters. The "follow us" links point to sister properties (La Tropical, San Juan 901), not El Colonial — guests trying to follow you land on a different business.

The fix. Replace them with El Colonial's own profiles (or create them) so every link points back to your hotel.

Google can't read your site as a hotel

High

Why it matters. No structured data — so Google can't show your 4.6 rating in hotel results, and AI travel assistants can't read you.

The fix. Add hotel tagging (name, rating, room types) so search and AI can understand and surface the property.

The travel sites outrank you for your own name

High

Why it matters. Search "El Colonial Hotel" and the TripAdvisor listing appears above your own site — so even guests looking for you land on a platform first.

The fix. A proper site with hotel tagging and direct booking pulls that top spot back.

Weak on mobile, and no website security in place

Medium

Why it matters. Mobile is where most travelers browse and your site scores poorly there (47/100); it also has none of the standard security headers.

The fix. A mobile performance pass plus standard security headers.

Also worth a look: respond to the recent 1★ and 2★ reviews; and consider moving off the shared group page onto your own domain.

If we worked together, here's the order

  1. Take back the booking. Add a direct booking engine so guests can reserve with you, not just the OTAs.
  2. Fix the dead ends. Repair the social links (point them to El Colonial) and answer the recent low reviews.
  3. Make Google & AI read you. Add hotel structured data so your rating shows in results and assistants surface you.
  4. Strengthen the foundation. Mobile performance, security headers, and a plan for your own domain.
