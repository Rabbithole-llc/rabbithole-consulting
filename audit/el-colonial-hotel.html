What needs attention — in priority order

Your booking experience fights the guest

Critical

Why it matters. Guests can book, but it's a multi-click flow that resets to the top and isn't seamless, so many bounce to an OTA — and you lose the direct relationship, the data, and the margin.

The fix. Streamline to a modern few-step booking engine (Cloudbeds, Little Hotelier, Lodgify) so the path from "I want this room" to "confirmed" is smooth on phone and desktop.

Google can't read your site as a hotel

High

Why it matters. No structured data — so Google can't show your 4.6 rating in hotel results, and AI travel assistants can't read you.

The fix. Add hotel tagging (name, rating, room types) so search and AI can understand and surface the property.

The travel sites outrank you for your own name

High

Why it matters. Search "El Colonial Hotel" and the TripAdvisor listing appears above your own site — so even guests looking for you land on a platform first.

The fix. A proper site with hotel tagging and a clean booking flow pulls that top spot back.

Not surfacing for the category travelers actually search

High

Why it matters. "Boutique hotel Old San Juan" doesn't return your page in the top 10 — so all the guests in the discovery stage of the trip don't see you.

The fix. Targeted on-page work plus the hotel tagging above brings you into that category result set.

Weak on mobile, and no website security in place

Medium

Why it matters. Mobile is where most travelers browse and your site scores poorly there (47/100); it also has none of the standard security headers.

The fix. A mobile performance pass plus standard security headers.

The page is too heavy for the way guests skim

Medium

Why it matters. ~17k words and 84 links on one page — most travelers won't scroll far enough to find the rooms, the booking, or the reasons to stay.

The fix. Trim, restructure, and surface rooms + booking near the top so the page works the way guests actually read it.

Also worth a look: a thoughtful public reply to each of the recent 1★ and 2★ reviews on Google.

If we worked together, here's the order

  1. Streamline the booking flow. Replace the clunky multi-step path with a modern booking engine so guests finish on your site, not on an OTA.
  2. Answer the recent low reviews. A measured public reply to each 1★ and 2★ on Google — recent low scores carry outsized weight in ranking.
  3. Make Google & AI read you. Add hotel structured data so your rating shows in results and assistants surface you.
  4. Strengthen the foundation. Mobile performance, security headers, and a tighter page so guests can actually find the rooms and the booking.
